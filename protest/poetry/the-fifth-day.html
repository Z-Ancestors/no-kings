The Modern Poem

This contemporary adaptation transforms the traditional "Fifth of November" warning into a critique of modern power structures, corruption, and democratic erosion.

The Fifth Day (2023)
Remember, remember, the fifth of November,
The Epstein treason and plot.
I can think of no reason why the Epstein treason
Should ever be forgot.
Guy Stalks, Guy Stalks, 'twas his intent
To traffic Queens and their Regiments.
ICE migrants to the state in tow,
To prove old Demos' overthrow;
By Don's epithets he was catch'd
With a dark spot and lines that match.
Holloa, toys! holloa, ploys! let the bells ring!
Holloa, boys! holloa, goys! Don ain't the King!
Original: Fifth of November (Traditional)
Remember, remember the fifth of November,
Gunpowder treason and plot.
I see no reason why gunpowder treason
Should ever be forgot.
Guy Fawkes, Guy Fawkes, 'twas his intent
To blow up the King and the Parliament.
Three score barrels of powder below,
To prove old England's overthrow;
By God's providence he was catch'd,
With a dark lantern and lighted match.
Holloa, boys! holloa, boys! let the bells ring!
Holloa, boys! holloa, boys! God save the King!

Hermetic Protest as Intellectual Jiu-Jitsu

This project occupies a radical space at the intersection of hermetic tradition and contemporary critique. Using steganography, double entendre, and the frameworks of academic rigor, it operates as digital samizdat— a coded payload delivered through the architecture of high-context intellectual polemic.

"The old poem celebrates the discovery of a physical plot to destroy a government in one day. Our project argues we are living through a non-physical, slow-moving plot to hollow out a republic, and it is being carried out in plain sight, legalized through complicit institutions, and broadcast on our screens."

The Three Pillars

I. The Digital Grimoire

The evidence layer—a curated collection of public documents, timelines, and data visualizations that map connections between power, corruption, and institutional capture.

II. The Somatic Experience

The emotional layer—art installations, augmented reality, and performance pieces that make abstract power structures viscerally felt.

III. The Counter-Plot

The action layer—tools for civic cryptography, digital self-defense, and distributed memetic resistance.

Pax Artificia

The framework revealing how algorithmic governance represents a new form of hegemony—where code is law, data is capital, and the "barbarian" is anyone who refuses participation.

The Tradition

Drawing from Dadaist fragmentation, Cold War samizdat literature, and Afrofuturist masking, this work uses obscurity and encoding not as obfuscation, but as precision. The message survives because it cannot be easily destroyed—it must first be understood.

Not a protest sign for the march, but a manual for those who architect the machinery of understanding itself.

For Whom?

This is not populist art. It demands literacy in political theory, rhetoric, and systems analysis. It will find its home in graduate seminars, conceptual art spaces, and the forums where high-level analysis and structural critique converge.

"It creates an in-group by design—those who can decode are those who can deploy."

Operating Principles

This project operates under the following constraints:

  • No Direct Attribution: The work protects its creators through layers of interpretation. Plausible deniability is structural, not accidental.
  • Multi-Level Reading: Every text operates on at least three levels—surface, structural, and esoteric. The casual reader sees satire; the informed reader sees analysis; the expert sees architecture.
  • Academic Rigor: All claims must be defensible through established theoretical frameworks. We distinguish between documented fact and satirical amplification.
  • Encoded Distribution: The work circulates through channels that require context to discover. It is samizdat for the digital age.
  • No Popularization: We do not seek mass appeal or viral distribution. The work is designed for strategists, not crowds.
  • Criticism as Collaboration: Serious critique is welcomed as part of the dialectical process. We expect to be called elitist, paranoid, and dangerous.
  • Evidence-Based Satire: The poem takes kernels of documented truth and processes them through rhetorical machinery to arrive at political conclusions.

These aren't restrictions—they're the source code of the project's power. The constraints enable the work to exist in contested spaces while maintaining deniability and interpretive flexibility.

Characteristics

This work is defined by what it is and what it refuses to be:

What It Is

  • High-Context: Assumes literate audience familiar with theory, history, and systems analysis. Requires a "decoder ring" of political and literary knowledge.
  • Hermetic: Uses coding and allegory as structural necessity, not aesthetic choice. The obscurity is the message's armor.
  • Institutional Critique: Turns the language of power back on itself. Uses academic frameworks as weapons.
  • Vanguard-Oriented: Activates strategists, not crowds. Creates an in-group through shared literacy.
  • Post-Truth Navigation: Designed for an era where truth requires archaeological excavation and competing narratives war for dominance.
  • Intellectually Dangerous: Makes visible the mechanisms usually left implicit. Shows how hegemony operates at the level of language itself.

The Rhetorical Arsenal

The work employs classical Latin rhetorical devices:

  • Parodia: Systematic mimicry for ironic contrast
  • Antanaclasis: Repetition with shifted meaning
  • Paronomasia: Strategic wordplay and punning
  • Amplificatio: Rhetorical escalation and intensification
  • Innuendo: Suggestion without explicit claim
  • Antimetabole: Reversal of structure to encapsulate argument

Cultural Position

It is protest as intellectual jiu-jitsu—using the opponent's force and framework against itself. Critics will call it elitist; advocates will call it necessary. It would not be a protest sign carried in a march. It would be a coded manual passed among the strategists in the back room.
