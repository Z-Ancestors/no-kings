Keep It Simple Stupid

The KISS Method of Calling BS

When you're drowning in narratives, frames, and competing claims, use this framework to cut through the noise. It's simple. It's powerful. It works because it always follows the money and the power.

①

Identify Narrative, Frame & Events

What is the story being told? What's the official frame? What events are being highlighted or hidden?

Example: "Trump's candidate won in a blowout"

②

Cui Bono et Cui Pecunia?

Who benefits? Who gets power, attention, votes? Who gets money? Who profits from this narrative being believed?

Example: Trump's candidate benefits from perception of inevitability

③

Invert Narrative, Reframe & Events

Now flip it. What if the opposite narrative were true? What events would matter then? What frame would make sense?

Inverted: "54% of Republicans explicitly rejected Trump's candidate"

④

Cui Bono et Cui Pecunia? (Inverted)

Under the inverted narrative, who benefits? Who gets power, attention, votes? Who profits from this truth being known?

Inverted: Anti-Trump Republicans benefit from showing their real strength

⑤

Compare the Gap: Donde Est Differencia?

Where is the difference? What gap exists between these two versions? That gap is where the BS lives.

Gap: Why hide 54% voting against if candidate is inevitable?

Why This Works

The KISS Method works because power always follows money and benefit. When you identify the gap between the official narrative and the inverted narrative, you've found where someone is lying—or where truth is being hidden.

It doesn't require fancy analysis. It requires: curiosity about motive, willingness to flip the frame, and the discipline to follow the money. Anyone can do this. Everyone should.

Quick Cheat Sheet

  • 🔍 Step 1: What's the story?
  • 💰 Step 2: Who benefits? Who gets paid?
  • 🔄 Step 3: Flip it. What's the opposite story?
  • 💰 Step 4: Who benefits from the flipped version? Who gets paid?
  • ⚡ Step 5: Where's the gap? That's where the BS is hiding.
